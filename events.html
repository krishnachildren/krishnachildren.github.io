Curriculum

·      The Concepts

o   Nature – Prakriti

What is the fundamental nature of this world? Electrons, protons, quarks or even beyond.

o   Living being – Jiva

Why is life so special? Stones do not inquire about the nature of the universe, but humans set up universities to learn about it.

o   The Supreme Lord – Isvara

What is the ultimate principle sustaining the universe? Is that accessible to us?

o   Time – Kala

From ancient sundials to Swiss watches, great minds, including Newton and Einstein, have been studying what is time. From another perspective, people talk of time management and timelines. But what is time?

o   Actions – Karma

Laziness is death, action is life. But not every action achieves reward. What is the art of right action that leads one to perfection?

·      The Principles

o   Cleanliness

Cleanliness is next to Godliness. What is external and internal cleanliness and what principles help us stay clean and pure?

o   Tolerance

Life offers unavoidable difficulties. Lessons from the life of Haridas Thakur, Jesus Christ and Prahlada to stand undeterred.

o   Choice

Every choice leads to consequences. An intelligent assessment is necessary to make wise decisions in life. Lessons from the life of Arjuna and King Bali.

o   Satisfaction

What gives one satisfaction? Is it his/her achievements or recognition from others or inner emancipation? What is the principle of simple living and high thinking? Lessons from the life of King Ambarish, the Pandavas, Hanuman, and the banana seller Shridhar.

o   Equal vision

How do we relate to other humans, and even animals and birds? How do wise people see others? Lessons from the life of the hunter Mrigari and the sages Valmiki and Narada.

o   Love of God

Who is our ultimate shelter? Who can we trust undoubtedly? Who can we love without any reservation? How do we express our love?

o   Acharya, the teachers

A wise person learns from the teacher. Even Newton values standing on the shoulder of giants. An Acharya shows light even in the stormy and dark situations. The relationship between the student and the teacher is founded upon trust and purity. Lessons from the lives of some of the great students such as Arjuna, Dhruva and Jiva Goswami.
